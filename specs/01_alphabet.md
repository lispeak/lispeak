## Vowels

Basic vowels are

| Symbol | Sound in English | Russian Equivalent |
| ------ | ---------------- | ------------------ |
| a      | father           | а                  |
| o      | open             | о                  |
| e      | bet              | э                  |
| u      | moon             | у                  |
| i      | pit              | и                  |

Adding `y` before the basic vowel we create a derivative vowel

| Symbol | Sound in English | Russian Equivalent |
| ------ | ---------------- | ------------------ |
| ya     | yard             | я                  |
| yo     | yoga             | ё                  |
| ye     | yes              | е                  |
| yu     | <b>u</b>se       | ю                  |

## Vowel circle

So vowels form a symmetryic circle:

```
 o  | ya |  a
----|----|----
 yo | i  |  ye
----|----|----
 u  | yu |  e

```

Oppositions in this circle are used for different syntax and semantic symmetries:

- `a` / `u`
- `o` / `e`
- `ya` / `yu`
- `yo` / `ye`

And the center `i` is used for neutrality
